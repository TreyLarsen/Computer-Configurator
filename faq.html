Frequently Asked Questions — Pro / 240 Hz Fortnite PCs

Recommendations for pro-level builds tuned for stable 240+ FPS, minimal input lag, and competitive consistency.

What target FPS should I aim for in competitive Fortnite?

For pro-level play with a 240 Hz monitor, aim for a stable 240+ FPS at 1080p. Prioritise consistency (minimal dips) over occasional frame peaks.

Which CPU and GPU classes are best for 240+ FPS at 1080p?

CPU: a high-clock 8-core (or better) CPU — e.g., Ryzen 7 / Core i7 class — for consistent frame pacing. GPU: aim for RTX 4070 Ti / 4080 or equivalent for headroom at 1080p competitive settings.

What graphics settings give the best competitive FPS without hurting visibility?

Use low/medium textures, disable motion blur and film grain, lower shadows, and use performance mode where available. Keep view distance balanced for visibility while maximising frame rate.

How much RAM and storage do I need for competitive play?

16 GB of fast RAM is the minimum for competitive builds; 32 GB is recommended if you stream or run background apps. Use an NVMe SSD to reduce map-load stutters and improve overall responsiveness.

Which monitor specs are important?

Prioritise a 240 Hz panel with 1 ms response time for pro play. Adaptive sync (G-Sync/FreeSync) helps with stutter; favour 1080p for maximum frame rates on 240 Hz displays.

How do I minimise input lag (mouse/keyboard)?

Use wired peripherals, set mouse polling to 1000 Hz where supported, disable V-Sync and use a frame cap slightly above your monitor rate, and enable raw input in Fortnite.

What about network and ping?

Use wired Ethernet, prioritise a low-latency ISP, connect to the nearest Fortnite region, and close bandwidth-heavy background apps. Consider QoS on your router to prioritise gaming traffic.

Do I need custom cooling or a higher wattage PSU?

Good airflow and a PSU with ~20–30% headroom are important for stability. For sustained high clocks and long sessions, quality air cooling or a 240/360 mm AIO is recommended to avoid thermal throttling.

Should I overclock for better Fortnite performance?

Mild, stable overclocks can help reach higher frame targets, but only if thermals and power delivery are sufficient. Test stability and monitor temps; prioritise consistent frame delivery over raw peak FPS.

Any Windows and driver tips for competitive play?

Keep GPU drivers current, enable Game Mode, disable background overlays you don't need, set Windows power plan to High Performance, and use manufacturer's control panel to prioritise performance profiles.
